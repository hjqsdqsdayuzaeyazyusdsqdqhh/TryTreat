Loyalty Cards vs Cashback Apps: Understanding Both Systems

Germans have no shortage of options when it comes to earning rewards on their everyday purchases. Two of the most popular approaches are traditional loyalty cards offered by individual retailers and cashback apps that work across multiple stores and brands. Both systems promise to put money back in your pocket, but they operate differently and deliver value in distinct ways.

Loyalty cards are retailer-specific programs that reward you for shopping at a particular store or chain. In Germany, popular examples include the Kaufland Card, the EDEKA Payback integration, and dm's dmCard. These programs typically offer points, personalized discounts, and member-only pricing that accumulate as you shop exclusively at that retailer.

Cashback apps take a broader approach, allowing you to earn rewards at any participating retailer regardless of which store you choose. Apps like TryTreat aggregate cashback offers from multiple retailers, giving you flexibility to earn rewards wherever you shop.

In this comparison, we break down the rewards potential, convenience, flexibility, and long-term value of each approach to help you decide which system deserves a place in your wallet and on your phone.

How Loyalty Cards Work in Germany

Loyalty cards in Germany typically operate on a points-based or discount-based system. When you present your card at checkout, the retailer tracks your purchases and rewards you with points, credits, or access to exclusive member pricing. The specific mechanics vary by retailer, but the core concept is the same: the more you shop at a particular store, the more rewards you earn.

Kaufland Card: Germany's most popular loyalty card with over 10 million users. Earns bonus points on select purchases, provides access to personalized digital coupons, and offers member-only pricing on rotating products. Points can be redeemed for discounts or partner rewards.

EDEKA Payback: EDEKA integrates with the Payback loyalty system, allowing shoppers to earn Payback points on every purchase. These points can be redeemed at any Payback partner store, providing cross-retailer flexibility that most single-store loyalty cards lack.

dm dmCard: dm's loyalty program offers bonus points on all purchases, with accelerated earning rates on dm's own-brand products. Points can be redeemed for discounts or free products from dm's range.

The primary advantage of loyalty cards is their deep integration with the retailer's pricing and promotion systems. Member-only discounts can be substantial, often saving 10-20% on specific products. However, the value is locked to that particular retailer, which can feel restrictive for shoppers who prefer to spread their spending across multiple stores.

How Cashback Apps Work in Germany

Cashback apps offer a fundamentally different rewards model. Instead of tying your loyalty to a single retailer, these apps allow you to earn cash rewards at any participating store. The process is straightforward: you browse available offers in the app, shop as you normally would (either in-store or online), and earn a percentage of your spending back as cash.

In Germany, cashback apps have gained significant traction as consumers increasingly value flexibility over single-retailer loyalty. Major cashback apps partner with hundreds of retailers across categories including groceries, fashion, electronics, travel, and dining.

The typical cashback rate ranges from 1-10% of your purchase amount, with special promotions sometimes offering even higher returns. Unlike loyalty card points, which often have complex redemption rules and expiration dates, cashback is usually paid in real currency that can be transferred directly to your bank account.

Key advantages of cashback apps:

  • Work at multiple retailers, not just one chain
  • Offer real cash rewards, not points with complex redemption rules
  • Provide automatic savings without needing to remember a physical card
  • Allow stacking with coupons and store loyalty cards for additional savings

Rewards Comparison: Points vs Cash

The fundamental difference between loyalty cards and cashback apps lies in the type of rewards they offer. Loyalty cards provide points, credits, or member discounts that are typically redeemable only at the issuing retailer. Cashback apps provide actual cash that can be used anywhere.

Loyalty card rewards:

  • Points accumulate slowly, often requiring months to reach meaningful redemption thresholds
  • Points may expire if not used within a certain period
  • Redemption options are limited to the retailer's own products or partner offers
  • Member-only pricing provides instant savings but only on selected products

Cashback app rewards:

  • Cash accumulates with every qualifying purchase across all participating retailers
  • Real money that can be withdrawn to your bank account or used for any purpose
  • No expiration on earned cashback (though minimum payout thresholds may apply)
  • Transparent savings tracking shows exactly how much you have earned

For pure financial value, cashback apps tend to deliver more flexible and immediately useful rewards. Loyalty cards, however, can offer deeper discounts on specific products at their partnered retailers, which may provide higher per-item savings for dedicated shoppers of a particular brand.

Convenience and Daily Use

When it comes to daily convenience, both loyalty cards and cashback apps have improved significantly in recent years. Digital loyalty cards stored on your smartphone eliminate the need to carry physical cards, and most German retailers now support NFC-based loyalty card scanning through their apps.

Cashback apps have the edge in terms of effortless operation. Once you set up your account and link your payment methods, the cashback accumulates passively with every qualifying purchase. There is nothing to remember at checkout, no card to scan, and no points to track. The savings happen automatically in the background.

Loyalty cards require more active engagement. You need to scan your card or app at every checkout, remember to load personalized coupons before shopping, and keep track of your points balance and expiration dates. For dedicated bargain hunters, this active management is part of the appeal. For casual shoppers, it can feel like a chore.

When you combine both systems, the convenience factor multiplies. Use your loyalty card for retailer-specific discounts and member pricing, then earn cashback on top of those savings through a cashback app. This stacked approach maximizes rewards while minimizing the effort required from you.

Verdict: Loyalty Cards vs Cashback Apps

Both loyalty cards and cashback apps offer meaningful rewards, and the best choice depends on your shopping habits and preferences. If you consistently shop at one or two specific retailers, their loyalty programs will provide deep discounts and personalized offers that cashback apps cannot match. The Kaufland Card, for example, delivers exceptional value for Kaufland regulars through its combination of member pricing and personalized coupons.

If you prefer flexibility and shop at a variety of stores, cashback apps provide more consistent and versatile rewards. The ability to earn cash at any participating retailer, without being locked into a single chain, makes cashback apps ideal for price-conscious shoppers who follow the deals regardless of which store offers them.

Our recommendation: Use both systems in combination for maximum value. Keep your loyalty cards active at your primary retailers for their exclusive discounts and member benefits. Simultaneously, use a cashback app like TryTreat to earn rewards on purchases at all retailers, including those where you do not have a loyalty card. This layered approach can reduce your total spending by 15-25% when combined with coupons and free samples.
